Workbook · pipeline · depth → motion → video

Turn a still image into a video

A staged pipeline: drop an image, extract its depth map locally (Depth Anything v2 running through the workbook's Candle-ONNX runtime), sketch motion arrows on the canvas, let Gemini draft a Veo-shaped motion prompt from the depth + your sketch, then ship it to Veo 3.1 to render the final video. Five cells, one path, ending with an actual video you can scrub.

Drop an image to begin.

1 drop · upload Source image
Drop an image or click to pick One image — JPEG / PNG / WebP. Pick something with depth (a landscape, a portrait, an interior) for the best demo.
2 candle · depth-anything-v2 Estimate depth (locally, in your browser)

Depth Anything v2 (small, ~80 MB) runs through the workbook's runtime — Candle compiles to WASM, decodes the ONNX model, and produces a depth map per pixel. The runtime is the only ML stack bundled in this artifact; no transformers.js, no ort-web, no external service. Foreground = warm, background = cool.

3 draw · motion arrows Sketch motion paths

Click and drag on the image to draw arrows showing where things should move. Multiple arrows are fine — different objects, different directions. Combined with the depth map, this turns into a 3D motion hint for the video model: "subject orbits left, camera pushes in, foreground tree sways."

4 openrouter · llm Compose the motion prompt

An LLM (configurable in ⚙) reads a description of the depth map stats + your motion arrows, and writes a tight Kling-shaped prompt describing camera move, subject motion, atmosphere, and pacing. You can edit the result before generating.

5 veo · image→video Generate the video

Sends your image + the prompt to veo-3.1-fast-generate-preview on Google AI Studio (configurable in ⚙). Veo Fast renders a 5-second clip in 30–60s; the workbook polls the long-running operation and embeds the result inline once Google's CDN drops the bytes.

Depth model: Depth Anything v2 (small) via @work.books/runtime-wasm's Candle-ONNX runner — model bytes cached in your browser after first download. LLM + video calls go to OpenRouter; set your key in the ⚙ panel. Video output is streamed by Kling and embedded inline.
